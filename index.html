About the Program

In the contemporary digital landscape and looking ahead into the future, the research in Microwave and Optical communication technologies play a pivotal role in shaping the way people connect, communicate, and conduct business. Microwave communication has been a cornerstone for long-distance data transmission, while Optical communication, with its high bandwidth capabilities, has become integral for the rapid exchange of vast amounts of data. Their operation spectrum has been triggered by recent technologies such as millimeterwave, 5G, beyond 5G, 6G, Terahertz (THz), metamaterials, beamforming, fiber optic sensing, satellite-optical remote sensing, under-water laser communication, photonic integration, artificial intelligence, etc., and has received tremendous response from both researcher and user community. Specifically, the recent deployment of 5G technology in India and the research requirement of beyond 5G technology under the Make in Inda flagship scheme of GOI, necessitates adequate expertise in the areas of Microwave and Optics. Additionally, optical remote sensing and under water communication, which plays crucial role in environment and security, also require tremendous research expertise. These technical advancements align seamlessly with India’s ‘Digital India’ and ‘Innovate India’ initiatives, accelerating the nation’s journey towards becoming a knowledge-driven economy. This proposed event will predominantly focus on the current status and future development of Microwave and Optical Technologies in India and world.

About the Department

The Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) was established in 1991. It offers undergraduate programs (4 years), postgraduate programs (2 years), an integrated M.Tech program (5 years), and doctoral programs. B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering is one of the leading undergraduate programs at the SRM Institute of Science and Technology, with a distinction for scientific rigor and development. This program is accredited by NBA and the Engineering Accreditation Commission (EAC) of ABET, USA (https://www.abet.org). The department features well-equipped facilities, especially the Center for Electronics Skill Development (CESD), which has an anechoic chamber for antenna characterization. The department has strong collaborations with industries and reputed institutes like the Central Electronics Engineering Research Institute, National Instruments, Samsung, CoreEL Technologies, Entuple Technologies (Synopsys), and Optithought. The department has signed a MoU with New Age Instruments, Universiti Tunku Abdul Rahman (UTAR) Malaysia, the Electronics Sector Skills Council of India (ESSCI), Nano-Academic Technologies Inc., Canada, Impulse Technology, Haryana, India, and Numergion PVT LTD, Pune for the joint product development and skill development of students and faculties. It has received significant research grants from DST under FIST, AICTE, DRDO, BRNS, ISRO, UGC, and SERB. The prestigious Visvesvaraya PhD scheme for Electronics and IT from Meity, GOl, is granted to full-time and part time research scholars. The faculty members of the ECE department are versatile in many diversified fields. They have good research potential, with 2254 research articles published in both Scopus and SCIindexed journals and 84 published patents and 38 granted to their credentials.
